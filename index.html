Dan Zhao

AI Research Scientist
Microsoft · New York University (NYU) · Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)

I am an AI research scientist. I previously worked at Microsoft Applied Sciences, where I developed AI-based experiences for Windows Copilot, Windows Agent Arena, and WinSpot. During my tenure at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, I helped pioneer Green AI (energy-efficient AI), focused on understanding and mitigating the energy impact of AI workloads spanning training through inference and deployment.

Work Experience

  • MIT CSAIL — Research Scientist (2023–2025) Affiliated research on AI + economics and supercomputing.
  • SandboxAQ — AI Fellow in Residence (2023–2024) AI for quantum chemistry & battery simulation; LLMs for security/cryptography; large quantitative models; agents for hypothesis generation and experiment design.
  • AI for Scientific Research — AI Research Advisor (2022–2024) Advised/volunteered on applied research projects (x-ray crystallography, imaging analysis, multi-modal sensor fusion) across multiple institutions.

Selected Publications

A Green(er) World for AI
Dan Zhao, Nathan C. Frey, Joseph McDonald, Matthew Hubbell, David Bestor, Michael Jones, Andrew Prout, Vijay Gadepally, Siddharth Samsi
Discusses practical directions for reducing AI’s energy footprint across training, inference, systems, and evaluation incentives.
From Words to Watts: Benchmarking the Energy Costs of Large Language Model Inference
Siddharth Samsi, Dan Zhao, Joseph McDonald, Baolin Li, Adam Michaleas, Michael Jones, William Bergeron, Jeremy Kepner, Devesh Tiwari, Vijay Gadepally
Benchmarks inference performance and energy costs for LLMs across GPU generations and multi-node settings, with practical deployment tradeoffs.
Sustainable Supercomputing for AI: GPU Power Capping at HPC Scale
Dan Zhao, Siddharth Samsi, Joseph McDonald, Baolin Li, David Bestor, Michael Jones, Devesh Tiwari, Vijay Gadepally
Studies system-wide effects of GPU power capping at scale, showing energy/thermal reductions with minimal performance impact in key settings.
Windows Agent Arena: Evaluating Multi-modal OS Agents at Scale
Rogerio Bonatti, Dan Zhao, Francesco Bonacci, Dillon Dupont, Sara Abdali, Yinheng Li, Yadong Lu, Justin Wagle, Kazuhito Koishida, Arthur Bucker, Lawrence Keunho Jang, Zheng Hui
Introduces a scalable benchmark for multimodal agents operating in a Windows OS environment, plus analysis of agent behavior and failure modes.
Layer by Layer: Uncovering Hidden Representations in Language Models
Oscar Skean, Md Rifat Arefin, Dan Zhao, Niket Patel, Jalal Naghiyev, Yann LeCun, Ravid Shwartz-Ziv
Shows intermediate layers can encode strong representations; introduces analysis tools/metrics for representation quality across layers.
VideoWebArena: Evaluating Long Context Multimodal Agents with Video Understanding Web Tasks
Lawrence Jang, Yinheng Li, Dan Zhao, Charles Ding, Justin Lin, Paul Pu Liang, Rogerio Bonatti, Kazuhito Koishida
Introduces a benchmark of web-agent tasks grounded in long video tutorials to test long-context video understanding for agents.
